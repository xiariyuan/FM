\section{Conclusion}
We introduced \textbf{Laplace-Guided Temporal Reliability Association (LTRA)}, a simple plug-in module for online MOT that answers a specific question: \emph{when should a tracker trust appearance?} By summarizing track history with multi-scale Laplace temporal signatures and converting temporal agreement into a reliability score, LTRA improves association without modifying the detector or retraining the ReID encoder.

The current proxy evidence already supports the central claim of the paper: once appearance is made reliability-aware, association metrics improve and ID switches decrease. The full benchmark campaign is therefore organized around a single, narrow contribution rather than a large multi-module tracking system. We believe this design leads to a cleaner scientific story and a more transferable improvement for strong tracking-by-detection baselines.
